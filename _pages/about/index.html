I research how algorithmic decision-making has impacted the norms of free speech and privacy. I am a rising 5th-year PhD student and Affiliate Faculty at the University of Washington School of Law. I conduct cross-disciplinary research with my advisor, Tadayoshi Kohno, at the Security & Privacy Lab and Tech Policy Lab.

I wrote an article called “Freedom of Algorithmic Expression” (University of Cincinnati Law Review, 2023), which explored whether social media platforms’ content moderation algorithms can be considered a form of speech under the First Amendment. I am currently working on “Generative AI and Law for Humanities.” My thoughts on Generative AI and Fair Use appeared in the Wall Street Journal.

Before arriving in Seattle, I was Deputy Director at the Ministry of Culture, Sports, and Tourism and the Korea Communications Commission from 2012 to 2019. I also interned at the Administrative Conference of the United States (U.S. federal agency) and the United Nations.
